Chandrayaan-3 is the third mission in the Chandrayaan programme,
a series of lunar-exploration missions developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
Launched on 14 July 2023, the mission consists of a lunar lander named Vikram and a lunar rover named Pragyan,
similar to those launched aboard Chandrayaan-2 in 2019.

On 22 July 2019, ISRO launched Chandrayaan-2 on board a Launch Vehicle Mark-3 launch vehicle consisting of an orbiter, a lander and a rover.
The lander was scheduled to touch down on the lunar surface on 6 September 2019 to deploy the Pragyan rover.
The lander lost contact with mission control, deviated from its intended trajectory while attempting to land near the lunar south pole, and crashed
